Opal: Designing Onboarding Users

Initial steps to logging in and signing up to be a member of the Opal Family on the dashboard.


OVERVIEW

Opal, “Tinder for Mentorship”, is an app that pairs college students with working professionals for mentorships. I was a part of the Dashboard team and created a clear user experience for admins' onboarding.

As one of the UX design interns for the Dashboard team at Opal, I worked on a variety of design projects during my year with the company. However, all the projects led up to my creation of the pages for the onboarding section for the Opal Dashboard.

ROLE

User Experience Design Intern

TOOLS

Figma

TIMELINE

June 2020 - June 2021



Initial Steps


Defining the problem and Initial Wireframes:

I was assigned to design the login and signup pages for onboarding on the Dashboard. Initially, when I received the user stories from the Product Managers, the task was to create a page that users would be directed to after receiving an access code to their email. Which is when I first designed these iterations:




Ideation


Concept Exploration:

However, after receiving feedback from the rest of my team, I was able to see that in these versions, the design was overpowering the content. I realized that it was ok to have white space and I decided to go back and design this page in a more welcoming and simple way.


Wireframing:

I created designs that were not only more simple and effective but also resembled the Opal mobile app with the ombre waves at the top and bottom.


Design Review:

However, after finishing these, the PMs decided to take a look at the visualization I had created around their user stories and decided to go back and rewrite the user stories. Instead of having these pages show up after clicking on a link in your email, they wanted them to be landing pages for anyone who was either signing up or logging in to their dashboard portal.


Competitive Analysis:

Before jumping back into the design process, I decided to go back to the drawing board and look at how other applications were utilizing the login and signup pages. This helped me better understand the visual hierarchy of such pages and gave me a better understanding of how to create a clear experience that would have an organic user flow.




Final Steps


Final Reviews:

While designing these final iterations of the onboarding pages, I made sure to connect the mobile app and Dashboard to create cohesion between these two products. This was also useful for the front-end developers to code the web pages as they would be able by to reuse the same code.


Final Deliverable:

My final product included four pages: one for logging in, two for signing up (one for your name and one to create a password), and one version which made it easier to switch between login and signup with a toggle.
