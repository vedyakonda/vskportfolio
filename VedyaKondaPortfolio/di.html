Digital Imaging

A medium for me to combine my passions for tech and art to tell my story.


OVERVIEW

I took a class titiled ARTS 74: Basic Digital Imaging at Santa Clara University taught by Professor Roya Ebtehaj. This class was an exploration of both fine art and commercial uses of digital media. Over the course of the quarter, I got hands-on experience with computer imaging and fundamental instruction in raster- and vector-based imaging software to manipulate photographs and create original imagery with the aid of Adobe Photoshop and Adobe Illustrator. Here are a couple of my favorite pieces I created during the course of the class.

TOOLS

Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator


01. Reflection


I used the two photographs on the left (which I took) as inspiration for my project below. I used Adobe Illustrator and the Abstract and Flat styles to create this project.

I wanted to use my self portrait to show how beauty is defined in our society in this day and age. I proudly wear my bindhi, but it wasn’t always easy. People made fun of me growing up for it. This is why I wanted to create an art piece that my younger self would have felt comfort in the meaning behind it. That is also why I chose to add text and the quote that says “Be your own kind of beautiful.”


02. Mind, Body, Soul


My project is based on the fact that I grew up during the digital era. As a part of Gen Z, we are constantly fed information whether we want to or not. Our thoughts and ideas make up who we are, and because of the Internet and especially social media, our beliefs are so structured around what we see on these platforms. It is almost like we are being controlled by our gadgets to see and believe specific things. Furthermore, as a Computer Science major, I am extremely fascinated by Artificial Intelligence, the ethical issues that come along with it, and how it is changing the world as we know it. I remember during my first quarter at SCU, I took CSCI 10 where Professor Navid Shaghaghi showed us The Matrix where an alternate reality is presented in which we are all hooked up to wires and are being controlled to believe and see the things that are taking place in our lives. Even though this exact scenario may not be true, our minds, bodies, and even souls are controlled by our gadgets and nowadays, we can’t even seem to do anything without them.

This is why I decided to convert this black-and-white self-portrait I had taken and overlay it with a photograph of wires I found on the internet using Adobe Photoshop. I then added texture which looks like a chip, binary lettering, and photo glitches to add to the overall meaning.”
